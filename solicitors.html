Common questions

Things Irish solicitors ask us first.

Will my callers know it's an AI?

Most won't. The voices are indistinguishable from a human receptionist for routine intake. We disclose AI status if a caller asks directly — never by deception. You can also configure the agent to introduce itself as a virtual assistant if your firm prefers full transparency.

How does this work with the Law Society's professional rules?

The system handles intake, not advice. It captures information and books consultations — exactly what a non-solicitor receptionist does today. We've built in safeguards around solicitor-client privilege, conflict checks, and data handling that meet (and in most cases exceed) what a manual front desk would do.

Is this GDPR-compliant? Where is the data stored?

Yes. By default, all caller data is processed and stored in EU regions. We sign a data processing agreement with you, the recordings are retained per your firm's policy, and the system is configured around the GDPR principles of minimisation and purpose limitation. We're happy to walk your DPO through the architecture before you sign anything.

Can it integrate with our case management system?

If you use Clio, Keyhouse, OSG, LawHub, LEAP, or any system with an API, yes — that's standard. If you're on something custom or older, we'll scope the integration during week one of the build.

What if it gets a call wrong?

Every call is recorded and transcribed. In month one, we listen to a sample of every call type and tune the system. From month two, edge cases get flagged for review. The agent is built to escalate to a human when it's uncertain — it would rather get a senior solicitor on the phone than guess.

Can we keep our existing receptionist?

Absolutely — and most firms do. The AI handles after-hours, overflow, and routine intake. Your receptionist handles the human moments that benefit from a human. Used together, your receptionist becomes more valuable, not less.
